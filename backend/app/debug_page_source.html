Correct %: (#Correct Questions in a Chapter)/(# Total Questions in a Chapter)

Accuracy: (#Correct Questions)/(#Attempted Questions)

Percentile: Percentile indicates percentage of students below given percentile value. For example, if you percentile score in 85%, it means 85% of the students have scored less than your score/p>

Graph - Marks vs. Rank

  • Use the interactive slider graph to find out the rank obtained by students at any score (See what would be your rank if you had scored 5 more marks).

Graph - Marks Distribution

  • This is a frequency chart informing about number of students (y-axis) who scored within a certain marks range (x-axis). It also indicates the median and average marks with the yellow and green flag (See definitions below).

  • You can seek following information from this graph:

    1. Know your competition: Find out how many students scored in a certain marks range. You can see how many students are ahead of you and lie in which marks range

    2. Benchmark yourself: See where you stand among all students with respect to median and average marks. You should always aim to be towards the right of average and median flag. See more details about median and average below.

    3. Difficulty Level of Test - Nature of graph indicate the difficulty level of test. More the area on the left side, tougher the test and vice versa (If more number of students are scoring low marks, it indicates that difficulty level of tests is high)

  • Median: Median Mark is the score obtained by the middle student. 50% students scored more than median mark and 50% students scored less than median mark

  • Average: Average Marks are calculated by adding all the marks obtained by all students divided by total number of students. Average Mark is a good indicator of mark obtained by a typical student in the test.
